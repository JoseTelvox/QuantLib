\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Forward rate agreement (FRA) class 


\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item Unlike the forward contract conventions on carryable financial assets (stocks, bonds, commodities), the value\+Date for a FRA is taken to be the day when the forward loan or deposit begins and when full settlement takes place (based on the NPV of the contract on that date). maturity\+Date is the date when the forward loan or deposit ends. In fact, the FRA settles and expires on the value\+Date, not on the (later) maturity\+Date. It follows that (maturity\+Date -\/ value\+Date) is the tenor/term of the underlying loan or deposit
\item Choose position type = Long for an "{}\+FRA purchase"{} (future long loan, short deposit \mbox{[}borrower\mbox{]})
\item Choose position type = Short for an "{}\+FRA sale"{} (future short loan, long deposit \mbox{[}lender\mbox{]})
\end{DoxyEnumerate}
