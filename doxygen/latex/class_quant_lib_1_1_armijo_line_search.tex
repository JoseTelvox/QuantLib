\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Armijo line search. 

Let $ \alpha $ and $ \beta $ be 2 scalars in  $ [0,1]
$. Let $ x $ be the current value of the unknown,  $ d
$ the search direction and $ t $ the step. Let $ f $ be the function to minimize. The line search stops when  $ t
$ verifies \[ f(x + t \cdot d) - f(x) \leq -\alpha t f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}(x+t \cdot d) \] and  \[ f(x+\frac{t}{\beta} \cdot d) - f(x) > -\frac{\alpha}{\beta}
    t f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}(x+t \cdot d) \]

(see Polak, Algorithms and consistent approximations, Optimization, volume 124 of Applied Mathematical Sciences, Springer-\/\+Verlag, NY, 1997)
