\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Zero-\/coupon interest rate swap. 

Quoted in terms of a known fixed cash flow $ N^{FIX} $ or a fixed rate $ R $, where\+:   \[N^{FIX} = N \left[ (1+R)^{\alpha(T_{0}, T_{K})}-1 \right] ,
\] with $ \alpha(T_{0}, T_{K}) $ being the time fraction between the start date of the contract $ T_{0} $ and the end date $ T_{K} $ -\/ according to a given day count convention. $ N $ is the base notional amount prior to compounding. The floating leg also pays a single cash flow $ N^{FLT} $, which value is determined by periodically averaging (e.\+g. every 6 months) interest rate index fixings. Assuming the use of compounded averaging the projected value of the floating leg becomes\+:    \[N^{FLT} = N \left[ \prod_{k=0}^{K-1} (1+\alpha(T_{k},T_{k+1})
                   L(T_{k},T_{k+1})) -1 \right],
\] where $ L(T_{i}, T_{j})) $ are interest rate index fixings for accrual period $ [T_{i}, T_{j}] $. For a par contract, it holds that\+:   \[P_n(0,T) N^{FIX} = P_n(0,T) N^{FLT}
\] where $ T $ is the final payment time, $ P_n(0,t) $ is the nominal discount factor at time $ t $.

At maturity the two single cashflows are swapped.

\begin{DoxyNote}{Note}
we do not need Schedules on the legs because they use one or two dates only per leg. Those dates are not adjusted for potential non-\/business days. Only the payment date is subject to adjustment. 
\end{DoxyNote}
