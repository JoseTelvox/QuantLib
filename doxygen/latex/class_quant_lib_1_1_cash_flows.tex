Cash-\/flow duration. 

The simple duration of a string of cash flows is defined as   \[D_{\mathrm{simple}} = \frac{\sum t_i c_i B(t_i)}{\sum c_i B(t_i)}
\] where $ c_i $ is the amount of the $ i $-\/th cash flow, $ t_i $ is its payment time, and $ B(t_i) $ is the corresponding discount according to the passed yield.

The modified duration is defined as   \[D_{\mathrm{modified}} = -\frac{1}{P} \frac{\partial P}{\partial y}
\] where $ P $ is the present value of the cash flows according to the given IRR $ y $.

The Macaulay duration is defined for a compounded IRR as    \[D_{\mathrm{Macaulay}} = \left( 1 + \frac{y}{N} \right)
